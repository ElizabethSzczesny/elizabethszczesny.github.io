DELIVERY TRACKING APPLICATION DESIGN
CASE STUDY

The challenge of this project was to create a delivery tracking flow for a logistics company. When tracking a package, especially internationally, consumers prefer as much information, regarding the whereabouts and timeline of delivery, as possible. The aim of this application is to efficiently provide these details in a reader-friendly format.

THE RESEARCH

I conducted primary research through several interviews. I interviewed a diverse audience, focusing primarily on individuals who had prior experience shipping internationally. It was almost unanimous that these consumers wanted tracking information that was detailed, accurate, and included updates regarding customs.
Taking this into account I identified important aspects of my design.

AREA 1

Must convey as much information as possible regarding the package’s location and delivery timeline.

AREA 2

Information must be presented in a readable format.

THE DESIGN

I knew the information I was presenting was straightforward, but would require an accessible site layout. I wanted the design to highlight the actionable feature - tracking.
I created the site architecture to make tracking information available at multiple points of entry.

This architecture can also be seen in the digital wireframes.

Usability studies with the prototype went well, and I began to create the final wireframes.

DESIGN SOLUTIONS

AREA 1

May be interested in the unique features the venue has to offer and how the space looks, so must convey those accurately in the design’s photographs.

THE SOLUTION

Both photo and video are offered to the user. Sliders and arrows indicate all the content that is available - a multitude of high quality images of the venue.

AREA 2

May be overwhelmed with planning, so lots of information and a planning system must be readily available and understood to aid in narrowing down choices.

THE SOLUTION

A planning spreadsheet beginning with clearly stated directions. This one spreadsheet is designed for complete planning with minimal steps:

  • Check date availability
  • Make selections for vendors
  • Choose the number of guests
Then, use the cost estimate calculator. This calculator can be used as many times as needed to configure different choices. Once your decisions are made you have the option to book the selections, make an appointment, or save the selections to an account.

AREA 3

May want to contact the venue for more information, so contact details must be easy to find and accessible.

THE SOLUTION

A readily available contact form along with contact details, such as phone number, listed at the bottom of every page.
