WEDDING VENUE APPLICATION DESIGN
CASE STUDY

The challenge of this project was to create a wedding planning application for a venue. The constraints users face when planning weddings are primarily those related to time. It is often difficult to manage planning a wedding on top of daily responsibilities and work. The aim of this application is to streamline the process and save the user time without having to sacrifice on a beautiful, individual experience and event.

THE RESEARCH

I conducted primary research through five interviews. I interviewed a diverse audience, including both genders and different adult age demographics. Common themes appeared throughout my interviews. My potential users almost unanimously wanted to see a large variety of easily accessed basic information online. As well as a wedding venue that made them feel welcome.
At this stage I also conducted a competitive audit. The competitive audit covered four competitors, two direct and two indirect. The user experience of each website was broken down into four areas: first impressions, interaction, visual design, and content.

I identified three specific areas to address in my design through this research.

AREA 1

May be interested in the unique features the venue has to offer and how the space looks, so must convey those accurately in the design’s photographs.

AREA 2

May be overwhelmed with planning, so lots of information and a planning system must be readily available and understood to aid in narrowing down choices.

AREA 3

May want to contact the venue for more information, so contact details must be easy to find and accessible.

THE DESIGN

From my research I knew I wanted to emphasize the idea of building blocks for a wedding. Each of these blocks can be interchanged, but used together they easily create a whole. This idea of creating a planning system grew into my design. I knew I wanted the ‘blocks’ to be apparent in the design. Look closely at my decisions for the landing page, and you will see block elements for the navigation, repeated twice, to underscore the idea, and a building block grid of images containing the wedding elements.

I conducted two rounds of usability studies. The early study helped me take the design from a wireframe to a high fidelity prototype that was easy to use and navigate. Only minor revision was needed by the time the high fidelity usability study took place.

I iterated on the designs several times following the usability studies. Notice when I create the planning and selection spreadsheet our 4 main blocks appear again. The chosen elements are placed in a final building block design on the final confirmation page.

DESIGN SOLUTIONS

AREA 1

May be interested in the unique features the venue has to offer and how the space looks, so must convey those accurately in the design’s photographs.

THE SOLUTION

Both photo and video are offered to the user. Sliders and arrows indicate all the content that is available - a multitude of high quality images of the venue.

AREA 2

May be overwhelmed with planning, so lots of information and a planning system must be readily available and understood to aid in narrowing down choices.

THE SOLUTION

A planning spreadsheet beginning with clearly stated directions. This one spreadsheet is designed for complete planning with minimal steps:

  • Check date availability
  • Make selections for vendors
  • Choose the number of guests
Then, use the cost estimate calculator. This calculator can be used as many times as needed to configure different choices. Once your decisions are made you have the option to book the selections, make an appointment, or save the selections to an account.

AREA 3

May want to contact the venue for more information, so contact details must be easy to find and accessible.

THE SOLUTION

A readily available contact form along with contact details, such as phone number, listed at the bottom of every page.

TAKEAWAYS

I believe this design accomplished its goal of streamlining the wedding planning process at a particular venue. By emphasizing the major components that go into wedding planning it creates an efficient, complete planning solution.
