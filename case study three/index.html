REDISTRIBUTION TO REDUCE FOOD WASTE APPLICATION DESIGN
CASE STUDY

The challenge of this project was to create an app for social good and a corresponding non-profit website. Aiming to reduce food waste and alleviate hunger in communities this app addresses the problem markets face when they do not have the logistical support they need to donate unsold produce rather than compost. The app is created to utilize the existing produce delivery routes and logistical system, leveraging these established routes and systems to simply accommodate one additional stop at a food pantry.

THE RESEARCH

For the beginning of this project I conducted secondary research. From a high level view this project was informed by reading about designing for social change. First, we must recognize the complex interplay between systems and humans. Every time individuals act they contribute to the running of the system in which they exist with their neighbors. Other aspects of designing for social change are agency, access, and action. More specifically, designing for access requires utilizing the tools and services readily accessible within one’s day-to-day life. Taken all together, we are trying to design for individuals by meeting them where they are, and giving them the power to take action easily. What does this mean for our app? Let’s take a look.
